# Design Inspiration

Websites that excel at presenting dense data in a readable, beautiful way. Collected as references for Observa's design direction.

## Current references

- **Our World in Data** — https://ourworldindata.org — Gold standard for open-data exploration: clean charts, long-form reports, excellent topic navigation.
- **Data Golf** — https://datagolf.com — High data density done right; packs a lot of numbers into tight, scannable layouts.

## Data journalism & visual essays

- **The Pudding** — https://pudding.cool — Visual essays that push the boundary of scrollytelling and custom interaction design.
- **Reuters Graphics** — https://www.reuters.com/graphics/ — News-driven data stories with polished, restrained chart design.
- **The New York Times Graphics** — https://www.nytimes.com/spotlight/graphics — Sets the bar for interactive data journalism; strong typographic hierarchy.
- **FiveThirtyEight** — https://fivethirtyeight.com — Clean statistical storytelling with signature chart style and transparent methodology.
- **The Economist – Graphic Detail** — https://www.economist.com/graphic-detail — One chart per day, always sharp, always opinionated; great economy of design.
- **Bloomberg Graphics** — https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics — High-end financial data storytelling; ambitious interactive pieces.
- **Financial Times Visual & Data Journalism** — https://ig.ft.com — Renowned chart style (the "FT pink" palette); excellent for time series and economic data.

## Data platforms & explorers

- **Gapminder** — https://www.gapminder.org/tools/ — Pioneered the animated bubble chart; excellent for exploring multivariate country-level data.
- **UN Data Commons** — https://data.un.org — Large institutional data explorer with clean, functional UI.
- **OECD Data Explorer** — https://data-explorer.oecd.org — Modern data browser; good model for faceted search over large indicator catalogues.
- **Eurostat Data Browser** — https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/ — Recently redesigned; relevant benchmark as a European statistics portal.

## Visualization studios & portfolios

- **Information is Beautiful** — https://informationisbeautiful.net — Award-winning infographics and data art; strong use of color and layout.
- **Flowing Data** — https://flowingdata.com — Nathan Yau's site; great range of chart types with tasteful, minimal design.
- **Datawrapper Blog** — https://blog.datawrapper.de — Not just a tool blog — their "Weekly Chart" is a masterclass in simple, effective charting.
- **Kontinentalist** — https://kontinentalist.com — Data storytelling focused on Asia; beautiful scrollytelling with cultural depth.
- **Visualizing Economics** — https://www.visualizingeconomics.com — Economic data visualizations with a clean, editorial feel.

## Product & dashboard design

- **Stripe Press** — https://press.stripe.com — Not data-heavy, but best-in-class web typography and reading experience worth studying.
- **Linear** — https://linear.app — SaaS dashboard that manages extreme information density with grace; great keyboard-first UX.
- **Nominal** — https://nominal.io — Engineering data platform with a very clean, modern data-dense interface.
