### Notes

- OECD is the strongest fit for a first prototype: it has direct productivity indicators, a dashboard, and a compendium geared to cross-country comparison.
- Eurostat is the clearest EU comparison fallback and explicitly covers productivity indicators in national accounts with data-browser/API access.
- ILOSTAT and World Bank are credible backups if we decide to use GDP per worker rather than GDP per hour worked, but OECD remains the cleanest match.
